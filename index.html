Welcome to my page.

I study decentralized collective action and governance in conflict and post-conflict settings.
My current focus is in the Southeast Asia, especially Myanmar.
My driving question is how decentralized collective action maintains the agency of actors, allowing them to take on incredible risks despite there being no centralized mechanism to curtail free-riding.
I study the institutional and normative devices which allow dispersed groups to coordinate governance and collective action despite personal costs.
